# Responsible Disclosure Policy

## Commitment

All security research documented in this repository was conducted through officially authorized bug bounty platforms. Every submission followed the program's defined rules of engagement. No systems were accessed without authorization. No user data was exfiltrated. No services were disrupted.

## Disclosure Process

A finding is added to this repository only after one of the following conditions is met:

- The platform has confirmed the finding as a duplicate, meaning it was already known
- The platform has marked it Not Applicable or Won't Fix, meaning triage is complete
- The vendor has resolved the issue and confirmed public disclosure is acceptable

Findings are not published here before triage is complete.

## On Duplicate Findings

Three of the ten submissions in this portfolio were marked as duplicates. This means the vulnerabilities were real, the reports were technically correct, and the PoCs were valid. Another researcher reported the same issue first. Duplicate findings are included in this portfolio because they represent legitimate security research conducted independently and to standard.

## On Not Applicable Findings

Five submissions were marked Not Applicable. In each case the behavior was identified, documented, and submitted through the formal process. The platform's triage team reviewed the submissions and determined they did not meet the program's threshold for action. These findings are documented here because the research process was complete and correct regardless of the platform outcome.

## On Won't Fix Findings

Two submissions were marked Won't Fix. The vendor acknowledged both findings and made a deliberate decision to accept the risk rather than remediate. These vulnerabilities exist in the application. They are documented here to show the full scope of findings, not just the ones that resulted in fixes.
